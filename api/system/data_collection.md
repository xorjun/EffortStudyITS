# 1. Information for participants

Thank you very much for your interest in our study on the topic “Learning Impacts of an intelligent tutoring system for programming (Version 2)”. Please consider the following information carefully.

## Purpose of the study

This study investigates which variation of the SCRIPT intelligent tutoring systems are most effective to support student learning.

## Responsible people for this study

The study “Learning Impacts of an intelligent tutoring system for programming (Version 2)” is performed by the research group Knowledge Representation and Machine Learning at the Faculty of Technology, Bielefeld University. The head of the research group is Prof. Benjamin Paaßen. The participating researchers are Alina Deriyeva and Jesper Dannath. During the study, only Jesper Dannath (who does not teach the course) has access to the server. Alina Deriyeva and Benjamin Paaßen will receive access after anonymization on March 31, 2026 (at which point the Introduction to Data Mining Course is concluded, as well).

## Study procedure

During the study, you are asked to

1.  Participate in a small personality survey (Big Five inventory)
2.	Participate in an initial test of your data mining knowledge
3.	Solve programming problems in the system until the mid of the semester
4.	Participate in a mid-semester test of your data mining knowledge
5.	Solve programming problems in the system until the end of the semester
6.	Participate in a final test of your data mining knowledge.

Note that all this content will be available for you whether you participate in the study or not. The only difference is that your data will be used for research if you participate. The exact data we record are:

-	Your IP address while you use the website (this is not coupled with any other information and will not be used for any scientific analysis but just to protect the technical functioning of the website)
-	E-Mail addresses (this is just for the registration process and is not used for any scientific analysis and not stored further)
-	Your rough personality variables (via an initial survey; Big Five Inventory)
-	Your programming behavior (that is, how your program develops over time),
-	When you used the ‘hint’ function, which hint you received and how you rated the hint.
-	When you click the ‘run’ and ‘submit’ buttons.
-	The system’s estimates whether you are struggling with a task or not.
-	How you navigate across tasks (especially when you click the arrow buttons on the top right)

We do not record data that could directly identify you and ask you to **avoid giving any hints about your identity** (e.g. via your user name or by including your name in your program code).

## Benefits and risks

By participating in the study, you contribute to research on the effectiveness of intelligent tutoring systems for programming, especially the SCRIPT system. Results of this study will, as such, benefit future students who use the system and scientific progress overall. 

Otherwise, we do not anticipate benefits or risks from participating.

## Voluntary nature of the study

The participation in this study is entirely voluntary. Teachers of the “Introduction to Data Mining” course have no access to the server data and have no knowledge who uses the tutoring system or participates in the study. You can use the tutoring system without participating in the study. You can withdraw your participation at any time until March, 31, 2026. Thereafter, the data will be anonymized and, thus, withdrawal is not possible. 

You can withdraw your participation by unchecking the corresponding box in the settings of the tutoring systems. If the box is unchecked at March 31, 2026, all data will be deleted.

## Privacy and usage of data

The raw data recorded during this study are treated confidentially. Details about data usage are explained in the attached privacy statement.

Study results will be published as scientific articles and presentations.

After the study has concluded, the anonymized version of the data will be published in a suitable research data repository. The purpose, type, and extent of usage of this data are not foreseeable at this time. Data will only be published in a form that does not permit any inference regarding individual participants. With this procedure the study is following recommendations of the German Research Foundation for quality control in research.

## 3.2. Legal basis for processing

The legal basis for processing person-related data is based on the participants‘ consent as described in Art. 6, 1a GDPR.

The legal basis for processing IP addresses is Art. 6, 1e GDPR to protect the technical functioning of the website.

## 3.3. Processing of person-related data and purposes

As part of the study, the following data is recorded and processed.

1.	For the registration process in the tutoring system: E-Mail addresses
2.	To protect the technical functioning of the website: IP addresses, pseudonym (username)
3.	For the purpose of answering the research questions:
    a.	Rough personality variables (via an initial survey; Big Five Inventory)
    b.	Programming behavior (that is, how your program develops over time)
    c.	Any clicks and key presses inside the tutoring system.

The person-related data are exclusively used for the purposes described here and will only be used inside Bielefeld University.

Recording is performed via the SCRIPT tutoring system and are temporarily stored on the system’s server at Bielefeld University. The only person with access to the server is Jesper Dannath. Before any scientific analysis, person-related data (e-mail, IP, pseudonym) is removed. The data on the server is wiped on March 31, 2026. Before publication, the data will further be checked for any accidental leaks of person-related data (as part of the source code). Only after this additional step will the data be considered anonymized and published.

## 3.4. Data transmission

During the study, only Jesper Dannath has access to the server. Alina Deriyeva and Benjamin Paaßen will receive access after removing the person-related data on March 31, 2026 (at which point the Introduction to Data Mining Course is concluded, as well).

## 3.5. Deletion deadlines

E-Mails are temporarily used for the registration process of the SCRIPT tutoring system and are not stored.

IP addresses are temporarily stored (for 24 hours) in the log files of the SCRIPT tutoring system.

Pseudonyms (user names) are stored on the SCRIPT tutoring system server until March 31, 2026.

All other data is considered not person-related.

## 3.6. Data publication

The presentation of results to third parties, be it in form of scientific publications or presentations, is only performed in form of statistically aggregated data or – if any reference to single cases is taken – in a form that does not permit inferences to the identity of single persons.
